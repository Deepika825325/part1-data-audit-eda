## Key Findings

### 1. Declining Customer Engagement Is the Strongest Churn Signal

Customer inactivity emerged as the strongest indicator of future churn.

| Metric                | Retained | Churned |
| --------------------- | -------: | ------: |
| Sessions (30 Days)    |     6.73 |    4.02 |
| Days Since Last Visit |     9.77 |   26.55 |

Churned customers visited the platform less frequently and remained inactive for substantially longer periods. This suggests that disengagement begins well before customers stop purchasing.

### 2. Low Purchase Activity Is Closely Linked to Churn

Retained customers placed an average of **5.18 orders**, compared with **3.04 orders** among churned customers.

Customers with lower purchase frequency were considerably more likely to churn, indicating that declining transaction activity is a strong early warning signal.

### 3. Campaign Engagement Matters

Customers who remained active interacted with marketing campaigns more frequently.

| Metric          | Retained | Churned |
| --------------- | -------: | ------: |
| Campaign Clicks |     0.74 |    0.55 |

Campaign interaction was approximately **35% higher among retained customers**, suggesting that marketing engagement can be used as a leading indicator of customer health.

### 4. Product Returns Contribute to Retention Risk

Average return rates were higher among churned customers (**8.24%**) than retained customers (**6.45%**).

Although the difference is smaller than engagement-related metrics, repeated returns may indicate dissatisfaction with products, fulfillment quality, or unmet customer expectations.

### 5. Premium Loyalty Membership Appears Beneficial

Customers in Gold and Platinum loyalty tiers demonstrated lower churn rates than non-members.

| Loyalty Tier | Churn Rate |
| ------------ | ---------: |
| Platinum     |     37.14% |
| Gold         |     40.75% |
| Not Enrolled |     48.34% |
| Silver       |     48.81% |

While higher-tier membership appears beneficial, Silver-tier customers performed similarly to customers who were not enrolled, suggesting that entry-level loyalty benefits may not provide sufficient value.

## Recommended Business Priorities

### Priority 1: Build an Inactivity Monitoring Program

Customer inactivity is the strongest risk signal identified during the analysis.

Recommended actions:

* Trigger re-engagement campaigns after prolonged inactivity.
* Send personalized product recommendations.
* Launch cart recovery workflows.
* Monitor customers with increasing days since last visit.

### Priority 2: Target Low-Frequency Buyers

Customers placing fewer orders were significantly more likely to churn.

Recommended actions:

* Deploy repeat-purchase reminders.
* Offer category-specific incentives.
* Introduce retention-focused loyalty rewards.
* Prioritize customers showing declining purchase frequency.

### Priority 3: Improve Loyalty Program Effectiveness

Higher-tier loyalty members demonstrated stronger retention outcomes than Silver-tier members.

Recommended actions:

* Review Silver-tier reward structure.
* Create faster progression paths toward Gold membership.
* Increase perceived value of early-stage membership benefits.

### Priority 4: Use Engagement Metrics as Early Warning Signals

Engagement indicators consistently outperformed support-related metrics in explaining churn behavior.

Key metrics to track:

* Days since last visit
* Sessions in the last 30 days
* Campaign click activity
* Purchase frequency

These metrics should form the foundation of future churn monitoring and retention dashboards.
